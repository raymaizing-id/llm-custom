## 🔬 TESTING PROCEDURES

### Raw Material Testing

**Physical Testing**:
- Appearance
- Color
- Odor
- pH
- Viscosity
- Specific gravity

**Chemical Testing**:
- Identity (FTIR, HPLC)
- Assay
- Impurities
- Heavy metals
- Preservative content

**Microbiology Testing**:
- Total plate count
- Yeast & mold
- Pathogens (E.coli, S.aureus, P.aeruginosa)
- Preservative efficacy

**Timeline**: 3-5 days

---

### Finished Product Testing

**Physical Testing**:
- Appearance
- Color
- Odor
- pH
- Viscosity
- Specific gravity
- Fill weight
- Packaging integrity

**Chemical Testing**:
- Active ingredient assay
- Preservative content
- Heavy metals
- Stability indicators

**Microbiology Testing**:
- Total plate count
- Yeast & mold
- Pathogens
- Challenge test (if needed)

**Stability Testing**:
- Accelerated (40°C/75% RH) - 6 months
- Long-term (30°C/65% RH) - 24 months
- Real-time (25°C/60% RH) - 36 months

**Timeline**: 5-7 days (routine), 6-36 months (stability)

## 📋 STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURES

### SOP 1: Raw Material Testing
1. Receive sample from warehouse
2. Log in LIMS
3. Perform testing per specification
4. Record results
5. Review & approve
6. Issue CoA or rejection
7. Update system

**SLA**: 3-5 days

---

### SOP 2: Batch Release
1. Receive batch record
2. Review documentation
3. Verify test results
4. Check compliance
5. Assess deviations
6. Make release decision
7. Issue CoA
8. Update system

**SLA**: 1-2 days after testing complete

---

### SOP 3: Deviation Management
1. Identify deviation
2. Document deviation
3. Classify severity
4. Investigate root cause
5. Determine impact
6. Implement CAPA
7. Verify effectiveness
8. Close deviation

**SLA**: 
- Investigation: 5 days
- CAPA: 30 days
- Verification: 90 days
